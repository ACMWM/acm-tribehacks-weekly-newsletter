Hackathons

Up To Code

Ferguson is holding their first hackathon in November. Up To Code will take place in Hampton, VA November 11-13. Registration will open on October 3rd, and is first come, first serve, so make sure to sign up early. The hackathon will be focused on home repair and improvements.


HealthHacks

VCU is holding (another) hackathon October 1-2. HealthHacks is a medical hackathon, specifically aimed at solving the problems faced by patients and hospitals. Groups are encouraged to be interdisciplinary, so this is a great hackathon to bring your friends from other departments to!


Talks

Challenges with Continuous Deployment

The Department is hosting a distinguished speaker talk with Laurie Williams of NC State on Friday, September 23rd in McGlothlin 20. The talk will discuss the challenges of implmenting continuous deployment, a system in which code is frequently tested and deployed to production environments.


The Suprising Resilience of the Patent System

The Law School is hosting a talk by the most-cited scholar in IP law, Mark A. Lemley. The lecture will be on September 26th at 12:50 in the Law School, room 127. If you're interested in the intersection of technology and law, RSVP here.


Jobs and Projects

W&M IT

The IT Department is looking for a new systems engineer. If you're graduating soon, and are interested in Unix/Linux, Git, Jenkins, Docker, and other technologies, check out the job posting here.


Entrepreneurship Center

The Entrepreneurship Center at the Mason School of Business is looking for a few web developers to work on the WM Entrepreneurship Page and to create a page listing the projects and startups of W&M students and alumni. If interested, please contact Christopher Hoyle at cthoyle@email.wm.edu.
